NX CAD/CAM


NX, formerly known as "unigraphics", is an advanced high-end CAD/CAM/CAE, which has been owned by Siemens PLM Software. In 2000, Unigraphics purchased SDRC I-DEAS and began an effort to integrate aspects of both software packages into a single product which became Unigraphics NX or NX.

It is used, among other tasks, for:

  • Design (parametric and direct solid/surface modelling)
  • Engineering analysis (static; dynamic; electro-magnetic; thermal, using the finite element method; and fluid, using the finite volume method).
  • Manufacturing finished design by using included machining modules.

It is an advanced high end solution for CAD/CAM/CAE. It is a direct competitor for software like: SolidWorks, Creo, CATIA, and Autodesk Inventor.


NX CAM after integrating with other NX solutions allows NC programmers & manufacturing engineers to associatively access complete designing, drafting & assembly tools in a solitary part manufacturing atmosphere.

The incorporated CAD/CAM capability allows one to handle design changes easily. User will be able to work with part models, create & assemble equipment, develop tool paths and model the entire machinery for 3D machine simulation in the specific environment.

The most powerful, flexible, and innovative product development solution in the industry, NX for Design has the features, performance, and capabilities to help you get product to market faster than ever before. NX for Design enables you to deliver products “right to market, first time" using more virtual product models and fewer, more costly, physical prototypes. This leads to market gains, lower development costs, and improved product quality.


Features & Objectives :

CAD (Computer-Aided-Design)
  • Parametric solid modeling (direct modeling & feature-based)
  • Freeform surface modelling (class A surfaces)
  • Reverse Engineering
  • Styling & Computer-Aided Industrial Designing
  • Engineering Drafting/Drawing
  • PMI (Product and Manufacturing Information)
  • Analytics & Reporting, Verification & Validation
  • Knowledge reuse, including knowledge-based engineering
  • Sheet metal design
  • Assembly modelling and digital mockup
  • Routing for electrical wiring and mechanical piping
CAM (Computer-Aided-Manufacturing)
  • NC (Numerical Control) programming
  • Robotics Programming
  • Solution Connectivity
  • Hybrid Additive Manufacturing
